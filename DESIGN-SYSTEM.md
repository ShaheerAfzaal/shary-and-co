# SHARY & CO — WEBSITE DESIGN SYSTEM (MANDATORY)

> When redesigning or rebuilding any page of the Shary & Co website, all design
> decisions must follow this design system. Do not improvise colors, fonts,
> spacing, proportions, or visual hierarchy unless explicitly instructed.

The website should feel: **Premium · Trustworthy · International · Elegant ·
Academic · Luxury education consultancy** — similar in quality to high-end
university recruitment websites.

It must communicate: _"Globally recognised education opportunities delivered
through a premium consultancy experience."_

---

## Brand Colour System

| Role | Colour | Hex | Purpose |
|------|--------|-----|---------|
| **Primary** | Scarlet Red | `#C1121F` | Primary CTAs, important highlights, active nav states, key trust indicators, hover states |
| **Secondary** | Warm Gold | `#D4A017` | Premium accents, icons, statistics, achievement indicators, borders, section highlights. Should never dominate — it exists to communicate prestige. |
| **Background** | Cream | `#FAF7F2` | Main background, content sections, cards, form backgrounds. Avoid pure white wherever possible. |
| **Text** | Charcoal | `#222222` | Headings, body, navigation, form labels, footer. Avoid pure black. |

### Colour Distribution — 60 / 30 / 10 Rule

- **60%** Cream (`#FAF7F2`) — primary page background
- **30%** Charcoal (`#222222`) — typography and structural elements
- **10%** Scarlet (`#C1121F`) + Warm Gold (`#D4A017`) — emphasis and conversion only

Never overuse red. Red should draw attention only to actions and important information.

---

## Typography System

**Primary brand font — Cormorant Garamond**
Use for: logo, hero headline, section headlines, pull quotes, testimonial
highlights, major statements. Elegant, premium, academic, prestigious — like
luxury publications and elite universities.

**Secondary brand font — Montserrat**
Use for: navigation, body text, buttons, forms, cards, captions, statistics, UI
elements. Modern, readable, professional.

### Hierarchy

| Level | Font | Size (desktop) |
|-------|------|----------------|
| H1 | Cormorant Garamond | 72–96px |
| H2 | Cormorant Garamond | 48–64px |
| H3 | Cormorant Garamond | 32–40px |
| Body | Montserrat | 18–20px |
| Small | Montserrat | 14–16px |

---

## Layout System

Apply **Golden Ratio** principles (1 : 1.618) wherever possible — hero split
layouts, image-to-text ratios, card proportions, section spacing, content width,
white space.

**Example — Hero layout:** Text area = 38%, Image area = 62% (instead of 50/50).

---

## Spacing System

Generous spacing; avoid crowded layouts.

- **Section padding:** 120–160px desktop
- **Content width:** max 1280px
- **Cards:** min 32px internal padding
- **Vertical rhythm scale:** 8 · 16 · 24 · 40 · 64 · 104px (avoid random values)

---

## Visual Hierarchy

Every section should clearly answer **one** question:

1. Why trust Shary & Co?
2. Why AMU?
3. What opportunities will I gain?
4. What is student life like?
5. What is the investment?
6. What happens next?
7. How do I apply?

Never overwhelm users with too many competing messages.

---

## Button System

**Primary CTA** — background Scarlet `#C1121F`, text Cream `#FAF7F2`, radius 12px,
subtle shadow, hover darkens red ~10%.

**Secondary CTA** — transparent background, 1.5px Warm Gold `#D4A017` border, text
Charcoal `#222222`.

---

## Card Design

Premium feel: cream background, subtle shadow, 16–20px radius, gold accent
details, generous padding, strong typography hierarchy. Avoid SaaS-style cards
with excessive borders.

---

## Imagery Direction

**Use:** real university & student photography, medical education environments,
laboratories, clinical training, graduation ceremonies, international students.

**Avoid:** generic stock photos, overly posed corporate imagery, cartoon
illustrations. Images should feel authentic and aspirational.

---

## Animation Rules

**Subtle only:** fade-in, fade-up, smooth hover effects, soft image zoom on hover.

**Avoid:** excessive motion, flashing, aggressive parallax, distracting animation.

---

## Overall Design Goal

The final website should feel closer to a **premium international university
recruitment website** than a study-abroad agency landing page. The visitor should
immediately think: _"This is a professional, internationally focused education
consultancy that works with serious students and respected universities."_
